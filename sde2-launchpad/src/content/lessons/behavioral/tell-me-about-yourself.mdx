This isn't your life story — it's a **90-second narrative** that positions you for the role. Done well, it makes the interviewer ask about exactly the things you're strongest in.

## The structure: Present → Past → Future

- **Present** (1 sentence): who you are now. *"I'm a full-stack engineer with ~2 years building React Native and Node.js products on AWS."*
- **Past** (2–3 sentences): the highlights that built you, chosen to match this role. Pick the 1–2 accomplishments most relevant to the job — with a concrete result.
- **Future** (1 sentence): why you're excited about *this* kind of role/company. *"I'm looking to take on harder backend and scale problems at a product company, which is exactly why this role caught my eye."*

## A worked example

> "I'm a full-stack engineer with about two years of experience, mostly building mobile apps in React Native backed by Node.js services on AWS. Recently I designed and built the async processing pipeline for [product] — using a queue, background workers, and S3 — which let us handle [X]× the load reliably. I really enjoy the systems side: making things scale and reasoning about tradeoffs. I'm now looking to go deeper on backend and distributed systems at a product-focused company, which is why this role stood out."

Notice: it's specific, it has one quantified win, it signals the direction you want, and it ends by pointing at *their* role.

## Do / don't

- **Do** tailor the "past" to the job description's keywords.
- **Do** keep it to ~90 seconds — practice with a timer.
- **Don't** recite your résumé top to bottom or list every job.
- **Don't** go personal/biographical ("I was born in…").
- **Don't** badmouth a current employer when explaining why you're leaving — frame it as moving *toward* growth, not away from problems.
